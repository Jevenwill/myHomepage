I'm convinced that the only thing that kept me going was that I loved what I did. Which is my favourite said by Steve Jobs, former CEO of Apple and a great man, and I was totally inspired by it. That's why I chose to change my major to CS after I'd realized what I really love to do is coding. One year ago, during my sophomore year, that summer has given me a lot of time to think and reflect upon my experiences towards majoring in Computer Science. Learning CS involves experimentation. I have to mess around with the code, really get into it. Sometimes it means that I'll break something that was already working. But the great thing is that in return, I get to learn something new, sometimes something that isn't even documented or fixed. This definitely happens to me at study everyday. Sometimes I feel like taking the lazy approach and just going back to the old ways of doing things, but trust me, it's definitely a lot better to just play around with it.
